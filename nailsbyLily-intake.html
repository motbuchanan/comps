6 What it costs

Real numbers, cheapest first. You quit a paid tool once when work slowed down, so I'd rather start you low and let you move up when the bookings justify it, not before.

A · Start free$0/mo

A free website you own, plus free booking software. This is real, not a trial.

  • Free site builder (you own the account and edit it yourself)
  • Square Appointments free plan for booking: real open time slots, calendar sync, reminders
  • No monthly cost at all if clients pay you cash or Venmo in person
  • If you take card or deposits through it: about 2.6% + 15¢ per transaction, and nothing else
The tradeoff: your web address looks like nailsbylily.wixsite.com and there's a small "made with" badge on the page. Everything works, it just doesn't look like you own the address.
A+ · Free, plus your own addressabout $1/mo

Same as A, but you buy the domain so it reads nailsbylily.com.

  • A .com runs roughly $12–15 a year, which is about a dollar a month
  • Everything else stays free
My honest rec if you want to spend anything at all. A real web address is the cheapest thing that makes you look established, and you own it even if you switch platforms later.
B · Look fully professional$16–19/mo

A paid site plan. No platform branding, your domain built in, still edited by you.

  • Wix Light is about $17/mo billed yearly, Squarespace about $16/mo — roughly the same thing
  • Clean address, no badges, more room for photos
  • Booking still runs through Square's free plan, linked from the site
Worth knowing: Wix's own booking add-on needs their $29/mo plan. Not worth it — free Square booking does the same job, so we just link to it.
C · Everything in one placeabout $24/mo

An all-in-one like GlossGenius: booking, deposits, no-show protection, payments, reminders, and a simple site, one login, no contract.

  • Closest to what you had before
  • Deposits and no-show fees are the real reason to pay for this
  • Cancel any time if things slow down again
My take: only worth it once you're booked enough that a couple of saved no-shows pays the bill. Don't start here.
